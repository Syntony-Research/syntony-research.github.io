Why Evaluation Reports Need Interpretation Layers

Evaluation scores rarely speak for themselves. Governance audiences need claims, caveats, and action relevance.

AI evaluation reports often provide test results without a durable interpretation layer. That creates a translation problem: technical readers may understand the limits of a finding, while executives and oversight bodies may see a score that appears more decisive than it is.

Interpretive reporting makes the translation layer explicit by separating what was tested, what was observed, what can be inferred, what remains uncertain, and what decisions the evidence can responsibly support.

The goal is not to simplify away nuance. The goal is to make nuance usable by the people who must govern advanced systems.
