Governance Lag Is an AI Risk Multiplier

Review cadence, escalation authority, and monitoring latency matter as much as model capability.

Governance lag is the gap between the pace of technical change and the pace at which institutions can observe, interpret, decide, and act. For advanced AI systems, that lag can turn manageable issues into strategic exposure.

The practical question is not simply whether a model is risky. It is whether the organization can notice relevant changes, route evidence to the right authority, and intervene before deployment pressure or external events close the window for action.

Useful AI governance therefore needs tempo discipline: clear review cadence, predefined escalation thresholds, and decision records that preserve why a risk was accepted, mitigated, or deferred.
