Biography

Technical safety work for institutions that cannot afford vague risk language.

Nathan Heath is Founder & Chief Scientist of Syntony Research and Co-founder & CTO of Sentinel Seal, an AI safety certification startup operating in stealth. His work focuses on adversarial evaluation, safety methodology, governance architecture, and institutional decision-making under frontier AI risk.

He earned an M.A. from The Fletcher School at Tufts University and spent six years at National Security Innovations, reaching Senior Research Scientist and holding a TS clearance. He has conducted red teaming for OpenAI, Anthropic, and Handshake, and advises organizations translating technical evidence into policy, controls, and board-level decisions.

Nathan is an Expert Advisor for Catastrophic AI Risk at the Cloud Security Alliance, contributing to STAR for AI; a GLG expert network member; an affiliate of the Rutgers AI Ethics Lab; and connected to CSET Georgetown through Duco. His fellowship and network affiliations include Truman National Security Project, BlueDot Impact, NextGen, and the Hertog Foundation.

He is a plenary speaker at IASEAI 2026 at UNESCO House Paris and is scheduled for a June 2026 speaking circuit including the Minderoo Centre at the University of Cambridge, UNIDIR in Geneva, and the American University of Paris.
