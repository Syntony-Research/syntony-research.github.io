01What it is

Evidence translation for AI risk work.

The model is being designed for a narrow problem: turning messy AI safety evidence into structured decision support. The point is not to produce a generic chatbot answer. The point is to help a team see what failed, what it implies, who owns it, and what decision now needs a record.

Finding packets

Convert traces, reports, eval notes, and monitoring signals into structured findings with severity, confidence, and uncertainty preserved.

Alignment implications

Map observed behavior to concerns such as tool misuse, oversight gaps, memory risk, benchmark gaming, and control evasion.

Decision records

Draft the record a governance owner needs: ship, hold, mitigate, monitor, escalate, or accept residual risk.

Control mapping

Identify the controls, owners, review triggers, rollback conditions, and monitoring requirements implicated by the evidence.

Client ecosystems

Adapt the workflow to each client's AI systems, vendors, risk appetite, governance structure, and operational evidence sources.

Audit trail

Keep the chain from evidence to finding to decision visible enough for later review, not buried in chat history.

02Why it matters

Most AI risk evidence dies in the handoff.

Red-team reports, eval traces, incident notes, and monitoring alerts often reach different audiences in different formats. Technical teams see behavior. Legal and policy teams see exposure. Executives see delay. Syntony Conductor AI is being built for the translation layer between those worlds.

For red teams

Move from “we found this behavior” to a finding packet that a governance owner can act on.

For governance teams

Understand which controls, owners, and review cycles are implicated by technical evidence.

For leadership

See the decision options, residual uncertainty, and record needed before a system moves forward.

03Release path

Coming soon, with client-specific deployments first.

The first deployments are planned as tailored workspaces for organizations with serious AI risk evidence already in hand: red-team findings, eval logs, policy exceptions, tool-call traces, monitoring alerts, or incident records. Public availability will follow only after the model, workflow, and review gates are ready.

This is decision support for AI risk work. It is not an assurance stamp, legal opinion, launch approval, or insurance score.
