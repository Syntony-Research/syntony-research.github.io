01What it is

What Conductor does.

Conductor is a specialized model for a specific problem: converting unstructured safety evidence into decision support. It surfaces what failed, what that implies, who owns the response, and what needs to be recorded.

Finding packets

Convert traces, eval notes, and monitoring signals into structured findings with severity, confidence, and uncertainty levels.

Alignment implications

Map observed failures to alignment concerns: tool misuse, oversight gaps, memory risks, and control evasion.

Decision records

Generate the decision record a governance owner needs: ship, hold, mitigate, monitor, or escalate.

Control mapping

Identify the controls, owners, review triggers, rollback conditions, and monitoring requirements implicated by the evidence.

Client ecosystems

Adapt to each organization's AI systems, vendors, governance structure, and risk tolerance.

Audit trail

Maintain a traceable chain from evidence to finding to decision.

02Why it matters

The problem: evidence that never reaches a decision.

Red-team reports, eval traces, and monitoring alerts reach different teams in different formats. Technical teams see model behavior. Legal teams see liability. Executives see delay. Conductor bridges these perspectives with a shared evidence structure.

For red teams

Turn behavioral observations into structured finding packets with severity, ownership, and recommended actions.

For governance teams

See which controls, owners, and review cycles are implicated by each finding.

For leadership

Review decision options, residual risk, and the audit trail before a system ships.

03Release path

Availability.

Initial deployments will be tailored for organizations with existing AI safety evidence: red-team findings, eval logs, tool-call traces, or incident records. Broader access will follow.

Conductor provides decision support, not legal advice, compliance certification, or launch approval.
