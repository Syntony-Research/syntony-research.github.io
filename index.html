syn·to·ny | noun

A state of resonance. Tuned to the same frequency.

In 1897, physicist Oliver Lodge patented syntonic telegraphy. The principle was straightforward and profound: tune transmitter and receiver into resonance, and meaningful signal emerges from electromagnetic noise.

In music, syntony names disciplined tuning relationships that make harmony intelligible. Syntony Research applies the same logic to AI: technical systems, governance institutions, and decision structures must be tuned into phase.
