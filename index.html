syn·to·ny /ˈsintənē/ noun

The condition of being tuned to the same frequency. Oliver Lodge coined the term in 1897 for syntonic telegraphy: a signal only works when transmitter and receiver are in phase. That's the problem in AI governance too. The technical findings, the legal duties, the operational controls, and the executive decisions are all on different rhythms. The work is getting them in sync before the system ships.
