syn·to·ny | noun

A state of resonance, tuned to the same frequency.

In 1897, physicist Oliver Lodge patented syntonic telegraphy, tuning transmitter and receiver to matching resonance so signal could emerge from noise.

In music, syntony describes precise tuning relationships. Our thesis is the same in AI, technical systems, governance institutions, and decision processes must be tuned into phase.
