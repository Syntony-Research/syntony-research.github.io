01 The Lab

"The most consequential AI failures will not be technical. They will be failures of translation — where what evaluators know never reaches the people designing governance."

Evaluation-grounded research that speaks governance

Syntony AI Labs develops open evaluation methodologies, assessment frameworks, and translational research designed to close the structural gap between AI safety work and the governance architectures that depend on it. Our research is informed by hands-on adversarial evaluation of frontier systems and published openly at peer-review standards.

Most safety research stays inside the technical community. Most governance work proceeds without it. Syntony AI Labs exists at the boundary — producing work that is methodologically rigorous and institutionally legible, so that what we learn from evaluating frontier models actually reaches the frameworks meant to govern them.

02 Research

Where we work

Safety evaluation, governance design, and the translation layer between them.

AI Safety & Alignment

Research on robustness, behavioral evaluation, and the properties of frontier AI systems that matter most for safe deployment — translated into frameworks that governance communities can act on.

Evaluation Methodology

Developing open, reproducible evaluation protocols and adversarial testing frameworks — including automated model-on-model red teaming and structured assessment methodologies for frontier systems.

Governance & Institutional Design

Translating evaluation findings and technical safety research into governance frameworks, regulatory architecture, and institutional design recommendations — informed by comparative international analysis.

Synthetic Media & Evidentiary Integrity

Assessing the impact of generative AI on testimony archives, institutional trust, and evidentiary systems. Home of the Testimony Archive Integrity Assessment Framework (TAIAF).

International AI Coordination

Research and engagement on multilateral AI governance — examining how international institutions, bilateral mechanisms, and norm-setting bodies can coordinate on frontier AI oversight.

Research Translation

The systematic practice of making technical AI safety research legible to policy communities, and governance requirements legible to the technical community. This is not an add-on — it is the core methodology.

What evaluators learn must reach the people designing governance. That translation is the work.

04 Approach

How the lab operates

Syntony AI Labs publishes open research at the intersection of AI safety evaluation and governance. Every framework, tool, and publication is designed to be used, tested, and built upon.

Open-Source by Default

All frameworks, evaluation protocols, and publications released under open license. Reproducibility is a requirement, not a feature.

Evaluation-Informed

Research grounded in hands-on adversarial testing of frontier systems — not theoretical speculation about models the author has never evaluated.

Publication-Grade Rigor

Formal methodology, transparent assumptions, honest uncertainty quantification. Designed to withstand peer review at top venues.

Translation as Core Method

Every output is produced in forms legible to both technical and policy audiences. This is not outreach — it is the research methodology itself.

Internationally Oriented

AI governance is a cross-border coordination problem. The research accounts for that from the first line, not as an afterthought.

Independent

Aligned with the work, not with institutional pressures. Open-source research in the public interest.

Open research. Open tools. Open source. AI safety and governance research is a public good. Syntony AI Labs publishes accordingly — all work available under open license for the research community to use, extend, and challenge.
