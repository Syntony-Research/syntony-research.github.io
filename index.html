syn·to·ny | noun

A state of resonance, tuned to the same frequency.

In 1897, physicist Oliver Lodge patented syntonic telegraphy, a tuning method that aligned transmitter and receiver to resonate at matching frequencies, restoring signal clarity in an increasingly noisy electromagnetic environment.

In music, syntony describes precise tuning relationships that make harmonic structure intelligible rather than chaotic. Syntony Research applies the same principle to AI systems, we tune technical capability, governance institutions, and strategic decision pathways into phase.

02 · Methodology How We Work

Evidence over assertion. Systems over silos. Translation as method.

Every Syntony engagement is built on three operational commitments. They are not branding language; they are how the work actually gets done.

i.

Evaluation-Grounded

Our analysis begins with hands-on adversarial testing of the systems in question. We do not theorize about models we have never evaluated. Every claim is anchored in empirical observation, with methodologies designed for reproducibility and defensibility.

ii.

Systems-Oriented

Frontier AI risk does not live in isolated domains. We model convergent pathways across cyber, CBRN, governance, and information environments, surfacing the shared intervention points that domain-specific frameworks systematically miss.

iii.

Translational

Our deliverables are designed to move between technical and policy audiences by default. Findings reach the people who can act on them, in the format they need to act. Translation is not outreach; it is the methodology itself.

04 · Leadership The Practitioner Behind the Practice

Hands-on adversarial evaluation, grounded in international affairs.

Syntony Research was founded by Nathan Heath, a decision scientist and AI safety researcher with 14 years of experience at the intersection of geopolitics and emerging technology. He brings deep analytical experience across deterrence, alliance stability, and institutional risk to the most consequential systemic challenge of our era: the safe deployment and governance of frontier AI.

Nathan consults as an AI Safety & Security Researcher with OpenAI and Anthropic, where he conducts adversarial evaluation of frontier systems. He is an AI Evaluations Fellow with Handshake AI, an Expert Advisor to the Cloud Security Alliance, a Security Fellow with the Truman National Security Project, and a Technical AI Safety Fellow with BlueDot Impact. He previously served as a Senior Research Scientist at National Security Innovations and has worked at the U.S. Department of State, Hudson Institute, the U.S. House of Representatives, and Harvard Law School.

He holds an M.A. in Law and Diplomacy from The Fletcher School at Tufts University and studied Politics, Philosophy, and Economics at Oxford and diplomatic and business strategy at Harvard. His writing has appeared in War on the Rocks, World Politics Review, RAND Europe, PRISM, and The Washington Post.
