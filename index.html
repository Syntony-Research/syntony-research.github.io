syn·to·ny | noun

Syntony: tuning systems to the same frequency.

In 1897, physicist Oliver Lodge patented syntonic telegraphy. The principle was straightforward: a signal becomes useful when transmitter and receiver are tuned to the same frequency.

That is the practical problem in AI governance. Technical findings, legal duties, operational controls, and executive decisions often move on different rhythms. The work is to bring them into phase before the system is already in the field.
