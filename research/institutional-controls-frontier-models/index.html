Institutional Controls for Frontier Models

By Nathan Heath. A practical control architecture for organizations deploying frontier models.

Record

Category: Governance

Status: Research note

← Back to research

Abstract

This article outlines a practical control architecture for organizations deploying frontier models, including escalation triggers, audit pathways, decision rights, and accountability boundaries.

Core framework

Frontier model governance fails when controls are treated as documents rather than operating mechanisms. A workable architecture links model evaluations, deployment gates, monitoring, incident review, and executive authority into a single decision system.

The article distinguishes four control layers: evidence generation, operational gating, escalation authority, and accountability review. Each layer must specify who owns the decision, what evidence is required, what thresholds trigger escalation, and how exceptions are documented.

Implications

  • Evaluation results should map directly to governance controls.
  • Escalation pathways should be tested before incidents occur.
  • Board oversight requires concise risk signals and traceable decision records.
