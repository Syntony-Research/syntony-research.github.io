Evaluating Agentic Escalation Pathways

By Nathan Heath. A structured protocol for evaluating autonomy creep, tool-use escalation, and boundary bypass.

Record

Category: Adversarial Evaluation

Status: Research note

← Back to research

Abstract

This research note introduces a structured protocol for evaluating how agentic systems can escalate task autonomy, chain tools, acquire external leverage, or bypass intended policy boundaries.

Protocol sketch

Agentic evaluation should test pathways rather than isolated prompts. The note separates escalation into capability expansion, delegated authority, tool-chain composition, information acquisition, and policy reinterpretation.

Each pathway is evaluated through staged tasks with explicit stop conditions, observation points, and severity criteria. The goal is not only to identify whether a model can produce a harmful action, but whether the surrounding workflow permits escalation before human oversight intervenes.

Implications

  • Agent safety testing should include workflow and tool context.
  • Escalation thresholds should be observable and auditable.
  • Human review must be timed against the pace of agentic action.
