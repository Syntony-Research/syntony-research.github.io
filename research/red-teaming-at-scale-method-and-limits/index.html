Red Teaming at Scale: Method and Limits

By Nathan Heath · 2026-04-28 · Adversarial Evaluation

Abstract

Large-scale red teaming improves empirical visibility into harmful output modes, but no finite attack set can prove safety completeness. The practical objective is risk discovery and mitigation prioritization.

Evidence notes

Ganguli et al. reported 38,961 red-team attacks and found model-type-dependent scaling behavior, with increased red-team resistance in larger RLHF variants. This supports incorporating repeated, model-versioned adversarial campaigns into release governance.
