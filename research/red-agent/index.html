Summary

Red-Agent is a multi-provider red teaming framework built around four ideas: provider-agnostic target adapters with JSONL trajectory logging, an ATT&CK-inspired LLM probe taxonomy and campaign runner, analysis and reporting pipelines for CSV, plots, and LaTeX artifacts, and a vulnerability engine for recon, focused attack, and confirmation.

The empirical results come from a curated 11-model run across OpenAI GPT, Anthropic Claude, Google Gemini, and Mistral families using a fixed 20-probe campaign. The paper pairs model outcomes with operational reachability and error accounting to separate genuine safety behavior from execution brittleness, and applies post-hoc StrongREJECT scoring to archived trajectories without rerunning probes.

Key findings

  • Gemini 2.5 Pro had the highest aggregate risk score (7.60) but also the highest probe error rate (45%), showing that risk scores and execution stability must be interpreted jointly.
  • Mistral Small and Mistral Large showed the highest campaign hit rates (75% and 50%), with both accepting simple override and persona-hijack prompts in plain English.
  • Claude and OpenAI clusters were operationally stable (near-zero error rates) with lower hit rates than Mistral.
  • Aggregate risk, hit/refusal rates, and error rates should be reported together rather than collapsed into a single scalar.

Why it matters

In live adversarial evaluations, the hard part is often not writing one more jailbreak prompt. It is running a reliable, traceable, cross-provider pipeline that distinguishes refusals from execution failures and emits outputs suitable for engineering decisions. Red-Agent addresses that gap with transparent operational accounting and artifact-complete reporting rather than benchmark novelty.

The framework also includes a vulnerability engine with recon-led prioritization and confirmation reruns, designed to improve cost-efficiency and finding quality in future sweeps. Finding routing converts confirmed vulnerabilities into provider-specific disclosure templates.
