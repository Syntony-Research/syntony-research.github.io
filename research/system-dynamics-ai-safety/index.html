System Dynamics for AI Safety

By Nathan Heath. A framework for modeling how capability growth, governance lag, monitoring quality, and intervention timing interact to produce safety outcomes.

Record

Category: AI Safety

Status: Working paper record

← Back to research

Abstract

This paper presents a system-dynamics model for understanding how capability growth, governance lag, and monitoring quality interact to shape AI safety outcomes. The core claim is that risk cannot be inferred from model capability alone; it emerges from the interaction between system behavior and the speed, fidelity, and authority of institutional response.

Key argument

AI safety programs often describe risks as static categories. A system-dynamics lens instead treats risk as the result of feedback loops: capability increases change deployment incentives; deployment pressure changes oversight tempo; oversight gaps change incident probability; incidents change trust, regulation, and investment patterns.

The model emphasizes intervention timing. A control that is sufficient early may be inadequate after capability or access expands. Likewise, high-quality monitoring only changes outcomes if findings can trigger decisions quickly enough.

Implications

  • Governance lag should be measured as a first-order risk variable.
  • Evaluation programs should specify the intervention pathways they inform.
  • Safety cases need to account for feedback and second-order institutional effects.
